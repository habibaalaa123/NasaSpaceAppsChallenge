North America

NBE
Summer Glory: In June and July, North America's forests burst with life, absorbing more CO₂ than they emit, resulting in a negative NBE—nature’s victory!
Autumn Shift: As leaves fall, photosynthesis slows down, and CO₂ emissions rise in November, marking the highest NBE.

NPP
Spring Surge: From March to October, longer days and warmth boost plant growth, peaking in June and July.
Winter Hibernation: During the cold months, growth slows, leading to a dip in NPP, but spring brings a revival!

Egypt

Just like in North America, Egypt experiences a rhythm in NPP throughout the year. With the warm sun and seasonal rains, plants flourish during the growing season, reaching their potential when conditions are just right.
