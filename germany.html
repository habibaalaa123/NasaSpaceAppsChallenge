2005-2010:

"Back then, I was making big strides in renewable energy with solar and wind power. This really helped me lower my emissions. But it wasn’t all smooth sailing—I still relied heavily on coal, which made it hard to meet the Kyoto Protocol targets. This was a wake-up call for me, and it pushed me to work harder on cutting down coal use."

2011-2015:

"A major turning point came in 2011 with the Fukushima nuclear disaster. I decided to phase out nuclear power for safety reasons, but that meant turning back to coal and natural gas, which made my emissions go up for a while. Then came Dieselgate in 2015, when one of my biggest automakers was caught cheating emissions tests. This scandal really hit hard, but it led to stricter standards and pushed me to embrace electric vehicles more."

2016-2020:

"I made a lot of progress in these years. By 2020, renewable energy finally became my main source of electricity, which was a big win for me. But I had some tough moments too. In 2018-2019, the Hambach Forest protests showed the tension between coal mining and environmental protection. A large part of the forest was cleared for coal mining, which hurt my ability to absorb carbon (NPP) and made my emissions worse. It was a hard lesson, but I’m still committed to reducing my coal use and investing in renewable energy."
